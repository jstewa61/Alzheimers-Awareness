Living with Alzheimer's

An estimated 5.8 million Americans of all ages are living with Alzheimer's dementia in 2019.

Leading Cause of Death

Alzheimer's Disease is the 6th leading cause of death in the United States.

The Gender Gap

More women than men have Alzheimer’s or other dementias. Almost two-thirds of Americans with Alzheimer’s are women.

Daily Struggles

People with Alzheimer’s often find it hard to complete daily tasks. Sometimes, people have trouble driving to a familiar location, managing a budget at work or remembering the rules of a favorite game.

Dementia on the Rise

By 2025, the number of people age 65 and older with Alzheimer’s dementia is projected to reach 7.1 million — almost a 27 percent increase from the 5.6 million in 2019.

Mental Health Effects

Approximately 30 to 40 percent of family caregivers of people with dementia suffer from depression, compared with 5 to 17 percent of non-caregivers of similar ages.

The Cost of Dementia

Alzheimer's and other dementias are expected to cost about $290 Billion for health care, long term care and hospice in 2019.

Prevenative Health

Only 16 percent of seniors recieve a regular cognative health assessment during routine health check-ups.

On the Rise

Alzheimer's deaths increased 145 percent from 2000 to 2017.
